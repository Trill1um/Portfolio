What’s it about
Sustainable Development Goal 4
Education is a vital part of our society. It’s responsible for the progress and growth of a civilization. It changes lives, it opens doors, and it empowers individuals to reach their full potential. With education, barriers like poverty, inequality, and lack of opportunity can be overcome, paving the way for a brighter and more equitable future. Yet, it lacks inclusivity; it isn’t accessible to everyone. Some might even say it’s a privilege. Millions of children and adults worldwide are deprived of the opportunities that quality education can bring, held back by barriers such as poverty, conflict, and inequality. This is where SDG 4 steps in. It’s a global call to action, aiming to ensure inclusive, equitable, and quality education for all. By addressing these challenges, SDG 4 strives to create a world where learning is a right, not a privilege—a world where everyone has the tools to build a better future.
Once upon a time
How I Discovered a Passion
I didn’t care much about academics—gaming was my world. School projects and assignments? I’d do the bare minimum just to have more time for what I loved most. Academics felt like a distraction from my passion. Then one day, everything shifted. A random YouTube video about coding popped into my recommendations. I ignored it at first, but it kept appearing, so I gave it a shot—and I was hooked. I never imagined how exciting it could be to see text and numbers come to life. From that moment, I dove headfirst into tutorials, documentation, and modules scattered across the web. What started as curiosity quickly turned into a drive to learn and create. Looking back, I realize I wouldn’t be here without that one YouTube recommendation. It sparked a passion that led me to Computer Science, something I wouldn’t have discovered otherwise, especially since programming wasn’t part of the high school curriculum. The inclusivity of online resources—tutorials, free docs, and community support—gave me the opportunity to explore and grow, showing how accessible education can lead opportunities, much like the mission of SDG 4.
Once upon a time
What I learned
Looking back, I wouldn’t be who I am today without my experiencing high school. It was then when I discovered my love for science and math—subjects that I once despised. High school opened my eyes to the beauty numbers, and the way science explains the world around us. With the help of passionate teachers, supportive peers, and classmates whom I can share my interests and love for the subject with, I am able to pursue what I love to do. This newfound passion not only shaped my academic interests but also set me on a path that continues to drive me today. It’s telling what education can do. And it being accessible is a necessity.
