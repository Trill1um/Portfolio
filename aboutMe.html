Through the years
5 Years Old
If you’re wondering why the title is "5 Years Old" yet I have baby pictures here, it’s because this section covers the range from 0 to 5 years old, a time I don’t remember much of. However, I do remember exploring the fields in Atimonan, Quezon. I’d get lost in the forest, play with animals, and turn anywhere into my playground. It was a time when my curiosity was at its peak. Though it saddens me that so much has changed since then. Some parts of the forest are now gone, the nearby hill is no longer a hill, the rice fields have been halved, and my family’s ownership may be revoked in the near future. How times have changed.
Roots and roofs
Atimonan, Quezon (My First Home)
A place where I could find peace. Whenever I feel stressed or unease, I always feel the urge to return to where I came from—Atimonan, Quezon. Life was simple back then. Surrounded by nature, breathing fresh air, and having endless space to play. It’s a shame I couldn’t explore more as a kid. I can’t help but feel there are still mysteries waiting to be uncovered there.

A Silent Hero
I wouldn’t say I’m very close to my mom, and even less so with my dad, but I have a profound appreciation for everything she’s done for me. From the moment I was born to the day I moved out for college, she’s been there for me every step of the way. Her support hasn’t always been loud or obvious, but it’s always been present. In her own way, she’s shown that she believes in me and what I want to do with my life.

A Silent Powerhouse
Now, my dad wasn’t there with me for most of my life—not physically, at least. But in his own way, he’s always supported not just me but our whole family. He made the ultimate sacrifice by spending years away from us, working abroad to provide for us all. If it weren’t for his hard work and dedication, we wouldn’t be where we are today.
Three musketeers
Me & my Bros
Originally, we weren’t on good terms with each other. Someone was always crying, getting hurt, or even seeking revenge—even if the cause was just an accident. But I guess kids will be kids because now, we have a normal relationship. We help each other, share banter, and even teach one another. While our relationship isn’t perfect, it’s far stronger than before, built on mutual understanding and the lessons we’ve learned from all those childhood squabbles.
